Haputale

"Misty hill retreat with tea, hikes, and stunning views"

Haputale is a charming hill town perched on a mountain ridge, offering sweeping views of lush valleys and distant coastlines. With its cool climate and peaceful atmosphere, it's a haven for nature lovers. Surrounded by tea estates and forests, Haputale is perfect for quiet escapes, scenic walks, and exploring Sri Lanka’s highland beauty away from the crowds.

The town’s unique location allows for breathtaking sunrises and panoramic views, especially from nearby viewpoints. Its cultural richness, friendly locals, and colonial-era charm add to the experience. Whether wandering through tea gardens, visiting religious sites, or simply enjoying the misty mornings, Haputale welcomes travelers looking for serenity and authentic encounters in the heart of the island.

Lipton’s Seat

Lipton’s Seat, one of Sri Lanka’s most iconic viewpoints, offers a spectacular panorama over tea country. Named after Sir Thomas Lipton, who often surveyed his plantations here, the spot provides stunning views of multiple provinces on a clear day. The hike or tuk-tuk ride through scenic tea estates makes the journey to this peaceful viewpoint even more memorable.

Adisham Bungalow

Adisham Bungalow, a beautifully preserved English country house, is nestled in the hills of Haputale. Built in the 1930s by a British planter, it now serves as a monastery. The estate features lovely gardens, colonial architecture, and a tranquil setting. Visitors can explore its interiors, purchase jams made by the monks, and enjoy a quiet escape into the past.
